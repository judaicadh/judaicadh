**Charakteres** is a term used for ancient ritual signs. These magical signs appear in all languages, with inscribed artifacts (amulets), ritual instruction manuals, and literary sources. Researchers find them across the ancient Mediterranean worldâ€Šâ€”â€Š[The Charakteres Project](https://charakteres.com/), led by Kirsten Dzwiza at the University of Heidelberg, focused on Greek, Demotic, Latin and Coptic sources dated between the 1st and the 12th centuries CE.

Finding fragments with charakteres in the Cairo Geniza tells us how widespread magical practice was among Jewish communities, and how these signs crossed geographical and linguistic boundaries to be a part of different cultures. Many researchers have taken interest into the charakteres of the Geniza, paying particular attention to how these signs were used and for what purpose/function on these fragments.

Subject 11619493: ENA 3682, Library of the Jewish Theological Seminary

According to notes from the University of Cambridge University Digital Library, this is an amulet for Hannah bat Rebecca. Most magical fragments in the Cairo Geniza do _not_ name specific individuals, but those that do typically end consist of the name of the individual (in this case, Hannah) followed by the name of the mother (in this case, Rebecca).
